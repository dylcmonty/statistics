## Problem 4

In order to fly from Seattle to Akron, you either need to go through the Chicago O'Hare airport (called ORD) or through the Detroit Metropolitan airport (called DTW) or through the Denver International Airport (called DIA). There are 5 times as many Seattle-Chicago flights as there are Seattle-Denver flights, and 4 times as many Seattle-Detroit flights as there are Seattle-Denver flights. If you fly through ORD, there's a 30% chance that you'll board a twin-engine turbo-prop plane (and a 70% chance that you'll board a jet plane). If you fly through DTW, those chances are 40% and 60% respectively. If you fly through DIA, those chances are 10% and 90% respectively. Given that you have recently flown from Seattle to Akron and boarded a turbo-prop plane in the 2nd leg of your journey, what are the chances that you flew through ORD?
