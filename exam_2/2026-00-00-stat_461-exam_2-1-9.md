## Problem 9

South American Condors are known to have a (population) average wing-span of 8 feet with a (population) standard deviation of 1.2 feet. The wing-spans are known to be normally distributed. What are the chances that a randomly selected Condor will have wing-span (a) exceeding 10 feet? (b) at least 5 feet? (c) between 6 and 9 feet?
