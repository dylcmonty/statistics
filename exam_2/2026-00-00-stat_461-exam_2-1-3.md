## Problem 3

Every day, during his lunch break, an undergraduate student of probability repeatedly rolls a balanced die until an odd prime number turns up for the first time. If $X$ = the number of rolls he needs to get the first odd prime number, what is $E(X)$? Suppose that today he has already rolled it 6 times and hasn't gotten an odd prime number yet. What are the conditional chances that he will need at least 10 attempts (including the 6 he has already made) to be successful?
