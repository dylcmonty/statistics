## Problem 6

A bag contains 24 pears, three-quarters of which are Bartlett pears and one-quarter are Asian pears. Reaching into this bag, you randomly grab 5 fruits (all at once). What are the chances that a majority of them are Asian pears? Suppose you do this (i.e. reaching into the bag and grabbing 5 fruits) repeatedly and each time count how many of them are Asian pears (call it $X$). What is $E(X)$?
