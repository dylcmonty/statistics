## Problem 14

A colony of bacteria being cultured on the surface of a nutrient solution in a lab has switches between two states several times during a 24-hour period -- a dormant state (when it basically does nothing) and a reproductive state (when it undergoes rapid cell divisions to increase the colony size). If the proportion of time (call it $W$) during a 24-hour period that it spends in the dormant state follows a standard beta density with parameters 5 and 3, what are the chances that $W>0.5$? That $W<0.25$? What are the mean and the standard deviation of $W$?
