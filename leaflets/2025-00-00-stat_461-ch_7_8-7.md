## Problem 7

The HbA1C levels in human blood (HbA1C is an indicator of one's diabetic level) are known to follow a normal curve. An HbA1C level of at most 6.0 is considered safe. A random sample of 20 adults in the greater Akron area who have a 'sweet tooth' (i.e. who are addicted to eating/drinking sweet stuff) was taken and their HbA1C levels were measured, producing a sample average of 6.8 and a sample standard deviation of 0.72. At a 1% significance level, is there sufficient evidence to believe that the true, unknown population average HbA1C of the adult population with a 'sweet tooth' in the greater Akron area is unsafe?
