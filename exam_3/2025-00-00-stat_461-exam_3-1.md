## Problem 1

Identify each of the following statements as TRUE or FALSE:

(a) Two random variables $X$ and $Y$ are independent if and only if their joint p.d.f. is the product of their individual (or marginal) p.d.f.s; (b) For a discrete random vector $(X,Y)$ where each of $X$ and $Y$ takes two values ($x_1$ and $x_2$ for $X$, $y_1$ and $y_2$ for $Y$) with

$P(X=x_1 \text{ and } Y=y_1)=p_{11}$, $P(X=x_1 \text{ and } Y=y_2)=p_{12}$,

$P(X=x_2 \text{ and } Y=y_1)=p_{21}$ and $P(X=x_2 \text{ and } Y=y_2)=p_{22}$,

the random variables $X$ and $Y$ are independent if $p_{11}p_{22}-p_{12}p_{21}=0$; (c) If $X$ and $Y$ are two random variables, each having mean $\mu$ and variance $\sigma^2$, the two random variables $X+Y$ and $X-Y$ are uncorrelated; (d) If the correlation between two random variables $X$ and $Y$ is $r$, the correlation between $U=aX+b$ and $V=cY+d$ is $(acr+bd)$.
