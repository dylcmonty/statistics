## 7

The new management of a financial consulting company decides to take steps to reduce the amount of waiting time for its clients in the lobby before they are called inside the office to have a one-on-one session with a consultant. Before they took over, the average waiting time used to be 22 minutes. To check whether the steps they took had any effect, they recorded the waiting times of 15 randomly selected clients and found a sample average of 14 minutes. If waiting times are i.i.d. Exponential (lambda) random variables, what is a 95% confidence interval for the parameter lambda? Based on this CI, do you think that the population-mean waiting time has changed from 22 minutes?
