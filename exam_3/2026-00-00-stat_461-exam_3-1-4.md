## Problem 4

It has been long believed that eating oatmeal regularly lowers blood cholesterol. To test this claim, a scientist with the FDA measured the change in blood cholesterol levels of a randomly chosen sample of 25 people in a certain age-group (who ate oatmeal for breakfast for a month) and found a sample-average reduction of 15 points with a standard deviation of 6 points. Is it believable at a 5% significance level that the population-mean reduction in all people of that age-group is more than 10 points?
