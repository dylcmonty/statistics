## Problem 19

Let the random variables $X$ and $Y$ denote the lengths of life (in hundreds of hours) for components of type-1 and type-2 respectively for an electronic system. The joint density of $(X,Y)$ is

$$f(x,y)=\frac{1}{8}xe^{-(x+y)/2}$$

for $x>0$ and $y>0$. Find the marginal densities of $X$ and $Y$. Are $X$ and $Y$ independent? What are the marginal mean and variance for each of $X$ and $Y$? Find the probability that a component of type-2 will have a life length in excess of 100 hours.
