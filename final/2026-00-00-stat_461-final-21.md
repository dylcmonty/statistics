# Problem 21

To compare the efficacy of two well-known diabetes medications, Glipizide and Jardiance, with respect to a control group, a simple random sample of 18 diabetics were recruited in a study. Six of them were randomly chosen and assigned to take Glipizide once every day for 6 months, another six were randomly chosen and assigned to take Jardiance once every day for 6 months, while the remaining six were given a placebo once every day for 6 months. At the end of the study period, their morning, i.e. pre-breakfast, blood-sugar readings were taken. The data are provided below. Test at a 1% significance level if all three population mean blood-sugar levels are the same or not.

| Number | Placebo | Glipizide | Jardiance |
|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 1 | 137 | 128 | 119 |
| 2 | 161 | 139 | 112 |
| 3 | 155 | 121 | 123 |
| 4 | 179 | 146 | 108 |
| 5 | 144 | 151 | 128 |
| 6 | 150 | 125 | 132 |
