## Problem 1

The world population has recently crossed the 7.5 billion mark, but approximately 1 in 8 people in the world today remain undernourished or malnourished. If you randomly selected 120 people from the world population, what would be the chances that there were (a) AT LEAST 5 undernourished/malnourished people in your sample? (b) AT MOST 9 such people in your sample? (c) BETWEEN 10 and 20 (inclusive) such people in your sample? [Hint: Check if it looks like a 'coin tossing' problem with a large enough number of tosses. If so, approximate it with an appropriate density.]
